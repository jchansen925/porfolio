We help you to build your projects on the strongest foundation

Geologic Hazards Assessment (GHA)

One of the first steps in planning a development is to identify the potentialxe geologic hazards. A GHA by our firm does just that. Once the geologic hazards are known, we can investigate the best way to avoid, reduce, or mitigate the hazards.

Landslide & Slope Stability Investigations

Development in mountainous areas may encounter existing landslides, or new ones can form. We identify areas prone to slope instability and investigate all forms of landsliding, including; rock falls, debris flows, and lateral spreading and help plan site development accordingly.

Faulting & Earthquake Hazards Investigations

Risks from seismic shaking can be great, and developments within active fault zones are at risk from ground rupture. We will help you site your development to minimize the risk of damage during a large regional earthquake or local fault rupture.

image
image

Erosion & Coastal Engineering Studies

The shoreline retreats at different rates in different places, and sometimes it seemingly erodes all at once. Coastal protection structures such as revetments and seawalls require maintenance to be effective. Whether or not you have coastal protection, we will help you locate your improvements away from current and future shoreline erosion hazards.

Timber Harvest Assessments

Sustainable lumber promotes a healthy forest. Proper attention to areas considered for timber harvesting helps prevent erosion, landsliding, and sediment into nearby creeks. We assess harvest areas to help get the logs out with minimal impact to the area.

image
image

Leachfield Investigation & Septic System Design

The soil plays an important role in absorbing and treating wastewater and is a factor in proper sizing of your septic drainfield. An improperly placed or sized drainfield may create a nuisance or affect slope stability. We can identify and test a suitable drainfield area for your development. We can even design your septic system.
